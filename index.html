What Inspires Me

Step into a world where petals become landscapes and tiny creatures become the stars of the scene. At Melody Spring Macro Photography, I specialise in capturing the hidden beauty of flowers and the delicate insects that move quietly among them. From the soft textures deep within a bloom to the gentle shimmer of a bee dusted in pollen, my work reveals the extraordinary details most people never stop to see. Every photograph is a moment of stillness — a ladybird exploring the curve of a leaf, a hoverfly pausing mid‑flight, pollen grains glowing in warm morning light. Macro photography transforms these fleeting encounters into vivid, immersive portraits of nature’s smallest wonders.

I use a Panasonic Lumix G9 II as my camera — it’s a micro four‑thirds system making it lightweight and easy to carry. My main lens is an Olympus 90 mm f/3.5 macro lens which gives an amazing 2× magnification and is a joy to use. I also use its baby brother, the Olympus 60 mm f/2.8, useful for larger subjects. When light is poor I use an Olympus STF‑8 Macro Flash, whose dual heads can be moved for optimum lighting. I shoot stacks of 40 images using in‑camera bracketing, then edit with Lightroom, Photoshop, and the stacking software Zerene — which can be trialled and purchased here.
