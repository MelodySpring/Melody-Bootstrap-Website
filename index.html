Inspiration

/* SWIRLY GALLERY CAROUSEL 5 IMAGES *?

Step into a world where petals become landscapes and tiny creatures become the stars of the scene. At Melody Spring Macro Photography , I specialise in capturing the hidden beauty of flowers and the delicate insects that move quietly among them. From the soft textures deep within a bloom to the gentle shimmer of a bee dusted in pollen, my work reveals the extraordinary details most people never stop to see. Every photograph is a moment of stillness — a ladybird exploring the curve of a leaf, a hoverfly pausing mid‑flight, pollen grains glowing in warm morning light. Macro photography transforms these fleeting encounters into vivid, immersive portraits of nature’s smallest wonders.

I use a Panasonic Lumix g9ii as my camera it is a micro four thirds making it light weight and easy to carry. My main lens is an Olympus 90mm f3.5 macro lens which is fantastic to use as it gives an amazing 2x magnification if needed and is a joy to use. I also use its baby brother the Olympus 60mm f2.8 which is useful for larger subjects. When light is poor I use a Olympus STF-8 Macro Flash which is good as it is possible to move the dual heads to get the optimum flash. I use a Benro Tortoise TT0R03C mini tripod with a K&F Concept GD3W geared head which gives ultimate control of the positioning of my camera. I shoot stacks of 40 images using incamera bracketing. I then edit with Lightroom, Photoshop and the most amazing stacking software in the world Zerene ehich can can be trialled and purchased here .
