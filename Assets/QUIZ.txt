1. What does the aperture control in a camera?
A) Colour balance
B) Depth of field
C) Image sharpness
D) File size
Hint: Think about background blur.
Answer: B) Depth of field

2. What does a fast shutter speed help you capture?
A) More light
B) Motion blur
C) Freezing action
D) Softer colours
Hint: Used a lot in sports photography.
Answer: C) Freezing action

3. What does ISO adjust?
A) Lens zoom
B) Sensor sensitivity
C) White balance
D) Focus distance
Hint: Higher values help in low light.
Answer: B) Sensor sensitivity

5. Which lens type is best for wide landscapes?
A) Telephoto lens
B) Macro lens
C) Wide‑angle lens
D) Tilt‑shift lens
Hint: It captures more of the scene.
Answer: C) Wide‑angle lens

6. What does white balance correct?
A) Image sharpness
B) Colour temperature
C) Lens distortion
D) Exposure time
Hint: Think “too blue” or “too orange.”
Answer: B) Colour temperature

7. What mode lets you choose the aperture while the camera chooses the shutter speed?
A) Manual Mode
B) Shutter Priority (S/Tv)
C) Aperture Priority (A/Av)
D) Auto Mode
Hint: Great for portraits.
Answer: C) Aperture Priority (A/Av)

8. What is the rule of thirds used for?
A) Adjusting brightness
B) Improving composition
C) Setting ISO
D) Choosing lens size
Hint: Helps place your subject.
Answer: B) Improving composition

9. What does a tripod help prevent?
A) Overexposure
B) Camera shake
C) Colour shifts
D) Lens flare
Hint: Useful in low light.
Answer: B) Camera shake
