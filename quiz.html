Photography Quiz

1. What does the aperture control in a camera?

  1. Colour balance
  2. Depth of field
  3. Image sharpness
  4. File size

Hint: Think about background blur. Answer: Depth of field

2. What does a fast shutter speed help you capture?

  1. More light
  2. Motion blur
  3. Freezing action
  4. Softer colours

    5. Hint: Used a lot in sports photography. Answer: Freezing action

3. What does ISO adjust?

  1. Lens zoom
  2. Sensor sensitivity
  3. White balancen
  4. Focus distance

Hint: Higher values help in low light. Answer: B) Sensor sensitivity

4. What does the term “bokeh” refer to in photography?

  1. The colour accuracy of an image
  2. The sharpness of the subject
  3. The quality of the out‑of‑focus areas
  4. The amount of digital noise in a photo

Hint: Think about what happens behind your subject when using a wide aperture. Answer:The quality of the out‑of‑focus areas

5. Which lens type is best for wide landscapes?

  1. Telephoto lens
  2. Macro lens
  3. Wide‑angle lens
  4. Tilt‑shift lens

Hint: It captures more of the scene. Answer: Wide‑angle lens

6. What does white balance correct?

  1. Image sharpness
  2. Colour temperature
  3. Lens distortion
  4. Exposure time

Hint: Think “too blue” or “too orange.” Answer: Colour temperature

7. What mode lets you choose the aperture while the camera chooses the shutter speed?

  1. Manual Mode
  2. Shutter Priority
  3. Aperture Priority
  4. Auto Mode

Hint: Great for portraits. Answer: Aperture Priority

8. What is the rule of thirds used for?

  1. Adjusting brightness
  2. Improving composition
  3. Setting ISO
  4. Choosing lens size

Hint: Helps place your subject. Answer: Improving composition

9. What does a tripod help prevent?

  1. Overexposure
  2. Camera shake
  3. Colour shifts
  4. Lens flare

Hint: Useful in low light. Answer: Camera shake
