Photography Quiz

Photography isn’t just about pressing a button — it’s a blend of creativity, technique, and knowing how to make light work for you. Whether you’re brand‑new to the craft or already comfortable behind the lens, this quiz is a fun way to test what you know and maybe learn something new along the way. From aperture and shutter speed to composition and colour, each question explores a small but important piece of what makes a great photograph. Hover over each card for a helpful hint, then flip it to reveal the answer. No pressure, no scoring — just a chance to sharpen your skills and enjoy the process. Ready to see how well you know your camera? Let’s begin. Hover over each question to get a hint and click to see the correct answer.

1. What does the aperture control in a camera?

  1. Colour balance
  2. Depth of field
  3. Image sharpness
  4. File size

Answer

B) Depth of field

2. What does a fast shutter speed help you capture?

  1. More light
  2. Motion blur
  3. Freezing action
  4. Softer colours

Answer

C) Freezing action

3. What does ISO adjust?

  1. Lens zoom
  2. Sensor sensitivity
  3. White balance
  4. Focus distance

Answer

B) Sensor sensitivity

4. What does the term “bokeh” refer to?

  1. The colour accuracy of an image
  2. The sharpness of the subject
  3. The quality of the out‑of‑focus areas
  4. The amount of digital noise

Answer

The quality of the out‑of‑focus areas

5. Which lens type is best for wide landscapes?

  1. Telephoto lens
  2. Macro lens
  3. Wide‑angle lens
  4. Tilt‑shift lens

Answer

Wide‑angle lens

6. What does white balance correct?

  1. Image sharpness
  2. Colour temperature
  3. Lens distortion
  4. Exposure time

Answer

Colour temperature

7. What mode lets you choose the aperture while the camera chooses the shutter speed?

  1. Manual Mode
  2. Shutter Priority
  3. Aperture Priority
  4. Auto Mode

Answer

Aperture Priority

8. What is the rule of thirds used for?

  1. Adjusting brightness
  2. Improving composition
  3. Setting ISO
  4. Choosing lens size

Answer

Improving composition

9. What does a tripod help prevent?

  1. Overexposure
  2. Camera shake
  3. Colour shifts
  4. Lens flare

Answer

Camera shake
